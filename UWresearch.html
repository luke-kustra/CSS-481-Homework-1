Undergraduate Researcher at University of Washington

I worked on a multidisciplinary University of Washington research

team developing autonomous navigation software for a driverless all terrain

vehicle. My role focused on designing and implementing machine learning driven

perception and decision making systems that enabled the vehicle to detect

obstacles and navigate toward target locations in simulated environments.

I implemented object detection algorithms using LiDAR sensor data, allowing the

system to recognize obstacles and perform actions such as stopping, turning, and

route correction. While the physical vehicle was not tested directly, key aspects

of the vehicle and software stack were modeled and validated using ROS, enabling

realistic simulation of sensor inputs, vehicle behavior, and control logic.

To support model development and evaluation, I used PyTorch and datasets such as

KITTI to train and assess perception models under realistic conditions. Development

was primarily done in C++ and Python within a Linux virtual machine environment,

with close collaboration across disciplines to ensure proper integration between

perception, planning, and control components.
